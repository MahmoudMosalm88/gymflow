# Market Research — GymFlow Round 1

## Topic
Gym management software competitive landscape 2026.

## Covered in this round
1. Top global competitors and their current pricing / features / weaknesses
2. Smaller and newer entrants disrupting the space
3. Common pricing models and tiers
4. Feature areas where current tools are weak or missing capabilities
5. Market size and growth trend
6. Current positioning themes from competitor sites
7. Initial user-trust and review picture

## Core findings

### Market size and growth
- Cognitive Market Research sizes the global fitness software market at `USD 1.67B` in 2025 and `USD 5.07B` by 2033.
- The same source sizes the Middle East gym management software market at `USD 704.6M` in 2025 and `USD 1.24B` by 2033.
- Egypt is listed at `8.87%` of the Middle East market in that dataset.

Sources:
- `https://www.cognitivemarketresearch.com/fitness-software-market-report`
- `https://www.cognitivemarketresearch.com/gym-management-software-market-report`

### Notable pricing patterns
- Quote-led pricing is still common in older incumbents: Mindbody, Glofox, EZFacility, parts of Virtuagym.
- Transparent self-serve pricing is more common in newer challengers: PushPress, TeamUp, Pike13, Wellyx, Gymivida.
- Per-member pricing still appears in Zen Planner and TeamUp.
- Free trial usage varies:
  - Gym Engine: `14 days`
  - Gymivida: `30 days`
- PushPress is still the strongest true free-tier benchmark.

### Main capability shifts versus the older competitive doc
- Wellyx now clearly belongs in the top competitor set.
- PushPress and Zen Planner now publicly surface WhatsApp support.
- GymMaster has public Arabic-localization evidence.
- The old absolute claim that no competitor has offline-adjacent capability is no longer defensible: GymMaster and Wellyx now publicly document offline-adjacent access behavior.

### Strongest current trust signals
- PushPress: G2 `4.9/5` (193 reviews), Capterra `4.7/5` (185 reviews)
- TeamUp: G2 `4.6/5` (269 reviews), Capterra `4.8/5` (341 reviews)
- GymMaster: G2 `4.7/5` (19 reviews), Capterra `4.7/5` (165 reviews)
- Glofox: G2 `4.5/5` (143 reviews), Capterra `4.4/5` (344 reviews)
- Wellyx: G2 `4.6/5` (16 reviews), Capterra `4.7/5` (14 reviews), Trustpilot `4.2/5` (55 reviews)

### Strongest positioning themes competitors use
- “All-in-one fitness business platform”
- “Grow your gym / studio”
- “Access control + automation”
- “Branded member app”
- “Retention / engagement / automation”
- “Transparent pricing” when available

### Updated strategic read
- GymFlow should not frame itself as the only serious option anymore.
- The stronger, more defensible angle is:
  - Egypt-first
  - Arabic-first UX
  - local pricing logic
  - QR workflows
  - WhatsApp-centered operations
  - simpler self-serve setup than service-heavy or enterprise-heavy rivals
