## 📋 Backlog (prioritised)

### High priority

**Trainer performance**
- Answers: "Are my trainers earning their pay?"
- Owner language: which trainer has the best member retention? Which PT drives most upsells? Which coach's classes have the worst no-show rate?
- Demand signal: #1 most-mentioned gap across all G2 review threads. LegitFit markets this as a differentiator.
- Requires: PT session data linked to trainers (already exists in PT module).

**LTV per member (stat card, not full report)**
- Answers: "What is each member actually worth to me?"
- Formula: ARPM ÷ monthly churn rate. Data already exists.
- Gym Launch says it's the #1 strategic number for deciding ad spend and plan pricing.
- Could be a single stat card on the overview bar, not a new report tab.

### Medium priority

**Class / slot profitability**
- Answers: "Which class should I cut?"
- Hormozi's rule: 80%+ margin per session. Revenue generated minus instructor cost per time slot.
- Requires: instructor cost input from owner (new data field needed).

**Acquisition funnel (trial to paid conversion)**
- Answers: "Which ads bring members who actually stay?"
- Zen Planner calls "visit-to-membership conversion" and "lead-to-visit conversion" critical gaps.
- GymFlow has Referral Funnel but no full-funnel lead conversion from first inquiry to paid member.

### Lower priority (future)

**NPS tracking**
- Every industry benchmark article lists NPS as must-track. Predicts churn before it shows in numbers.
- Requires: in-app member rating prompt (new feature in member-facing app).

**Member progress / results tracking**
- Glofox identifies "lack of visible progress" as #1 reason members quit.
- Hormozi's methodology centres on tracking member results.
- Requires: weight/measurement/PB data entry (significant new data model).

**Revenue per square metre**
- VERVE Pulse lists as core KPI. Benchmark: $30–60/sqm standard, $80–150+ boutique.
- Requires: gym floor area input from owner (new settings field).

**Member segment comparison**
- Do 3-month prepaid members churn less than monthly? Do referral members stay longer than ad-acquired?
- Gap across the whole market — nobody does this out of the box.

## Key owner language (use in UI copy)

Owners say → NOT:
- "How much am I about to lose?" → NOT "revenue at risk"
- "Who's gone quiet on me?" → NOT "ghost members" (use internally only)
- "Who owes me money?" → NOT "failed payments"
- "What did my reminders rescue?" → NOT "WhatsApp ROI"
- "Am I growing or just replacing people?" → NOT "net MRR change"
- "Who only comes once a week?" → NOT "low visit frequency"
- "The leaky bucket" → their metaphor for churn
- "Quiet quitters" / "silent quitters" → pre-churn at-risk members
- Headcount first, percentages second
- Monthly cash flow thinking, not ARR/LTV abstractions

## The 90-day rule (flagship insight)
87% of members who experience structured onboarding remain active at 6 months.
1x/week visit frequency = 50% cancel rate.
2x/week = 25% cancel rate.
3x+/week = <5% cancel rate.
GymFlow's Onboarding Performance report is the only tool that measures whether a gym's 90-day window is working.
